Tonight: Stock Up in SLC

You've got a 4-hour drive to Zion tomorrow morning. Tonight is for gear checks and provisions. Smith's Marketplace in Draper is the move — grab trail snacks, packed lunches for the first few days, and a case of water. REI Salt Lake is 20 minutes north if you need anything last-minute.

Day 2 SLC → Zion

Friday, March 27

Big views, small effort. First day magic.

🏡 Zion VRBO · Check-in · 3 nights
4-Hour Drive via I-15 South → Hwy 9 East

Enter Through the East Entrance — Worth the Extra Miles

Skip the main South Entrance and come in from the east on Hwy 9. You'll pass through the Zion-Mt. Carmel Tunnel — 1.1 miles carved through solid sandstone — and emerge into the canyon with Checkerboard Mesa's crosshatched rock on your left and your first true canyon views dead ahead. This is the arrival the park deserves.

🪨 Checkerboard Mesa — East Entrance pull-off 🚇 Zion-Mt. Carmel Tunnel — 1.1 mi of carved rock 👁️ Canyon Junction Bridge — first canyon panorama
Morning

Scout Lookout Trail

3.6 mi Moderate ⚡ Permit = Summit

The switchback climb through Walter's Wiggles — 21 tight stone zigzags hand-cut into the cliff — leads to the flat mesa of Scout Lookout, perched directly above Zion Canyon. The views from the top are staggering. If Nick's lottery permit comes through, the chains to Angels Landing summit begin right here.

Afternoon

Watchman Trail

3.3 mi Moderate

A perfect recovery hike — loops up to a mesa with panoramic views of the Watchman formation and the Virgin River valley glowing in afternoon light. One of the best sunset perches in the park. Start by 3 PM to catch the light at its best.

View on AllTrails →
🎫

Angels Landing Permit: Required via recreation.gov lottery. Day-before and day-of lotteries run daily — results post at 3 PM. If Nick hasn't entered yet, go now: recreation.gov/permits/4675315. Scout Lookout is a spectacular endpoint regardless.

🚌

Private vehicles cannot drive Zion Canyon Scenic Drive in season. Park at the Visitor Center or Human History Museum lot and take the free shuttle — runs every 7 minutes at peak season. Human History Museum lot is less crowded.

Day 3 Stay in Zion

Saturday, March 28

High intensity, iconic Zion.

Full Morning (Go As Far As You Want)

The Narrows

5.4 mi+ Strenuous

Wading upstream through the Virgin River inside a 2,000-foot slot canyon — one of the most surreal hikes on earth. The first mile is the most spectacular. Push to Wall Street (1.5 mi in) for the narrowest, most cathedral-like section. You don't have to go the full distance to be completely blown away. In late March, water runs cold and thigh-deep in sections — dry suits are mandatory.

View on AllTrails →
Afternoon

Temple of Sinawava

Shuttle Stop 9 Easy

The natural amphitheater at the end of the shuttle line where the Narrows begins. Return here dry in the afternoon — the scale of the canyon walls reads completely differently when you're not mid-river. Riverside Walk (1.1 mi paved, completely flat) connects it seamlessly. Extraordinary late-day light on the canyon walls.

View on AllTrails →
🤿

Rent dry suits — this is non-negotiable in March. Water temps run 40–50°F. Without a dry suit, neoprene socks, and canyoneering shoes, you risk hypothermia within an hour. Zion Outfitter and Zion Adventure Company both rent full packages (~$45/person). Reserve online before you arrive in Springdale.

🌊

Check flash flood conditions the morning of at zionpark.net or call the Zion recorded conditions line. The Narrows closes with zero notice when upstream weather threatens. Backup plan: Emerald Pools loop (Shuttle Stop 5) is excellent and keeps everyone happy.

Day 4 Stay in Zion

Sunday, March 29

Chill easy day — flat, paved, and beautiful.

Morning

Riverside Walk + Emerald Falls

2.2 mi Easy

The gentlest walk in Zion — fully paved, stroller-accessible, and completely beautiful. Follows the Virgin River through overhanging canyon walls to the mouth of the Narrows. Add a side detour to Emerald Falls for the canyon's best spring waterfall, especially lush in late March when snowmelt is running.

Afternoon + Sunset

Pa'rus Trail + Hot Springs

Flex Easy

Pa'rus is a 1.7-mile paved trail hugging the Virgin River — perfect for golden hour when the canyon walls go deep orange and the cottonwoods glow. After the sun drops, search "Zion Hot Springs" for the natural hot springs near the park boundary. A short hike, a surreal soak, and a memory the family won't shake.

Before You Leave Zion Tomorrow

Weeping Rock — Don't Drive Past It

Shuttle Stop 7. A 10-minute walk to a dripping alcove where groundwater seeps through the canyon wall, feeding hanging gardens of columbine and maidenhair fern. Free, fast, and completely otherworldly. Younger kids especially love the dripping ceiling.

Day 5 Zion → Bryce · 2.5 hrs

Monday, March 30

Check-in at Bryce Lodge. First hoodoos tonight.

🏨 Bryce Lodge · Mom's Reservation · 2 nights
~2.5 Hours via Hwy 9 → US-89 North → Hwy 12

Stop at Red Canyon — Almost Nobody Does

Head north on US-89 through Kanab and Panguitch. Just before Bryce on Hwy 12, you'll pass through Red Canyon — a free, pull-off hoodoo landscape that looks almost identical to Bryce but has basically zero people. Stop for 20 minutes and wander. It's stunning. Then continue to the park.

🟠 Red Canyon — FREE hoodoos, Hwy 12 pull-off 🏙️ Panguitch — historic main street, good lunch stop 🌵 Coral Pink Sand Dunes — side detour if time allows 🦌 Watch for pronghorn antelope on Hwy 89
Afternoon (After Check-In)

The Rim Trail

1.0 mi Easy

A short stroll along the canyon rim between Sunrise and Sunset Points. Look DOWN into the amphitheater from above — this is how Bryce is designed to be seen first. Get your bearings before tomorrow's descent into the hoodoos themselves.

View on AllTrails →
Evening

Sunset Point — Thor's Hammer

Overlook No hike needed

Stand at Sunset Point as the light turns the hoodoos from orange to blood-red to deep violet. Thor's Hammer — the park's iconic formation — catches the last rays perfectly. Arrive 30 minutes before sunset and find a spot on the rim. This one hits differently than any photograph can prepare you for.

🌡️

Bryce sits at 8,000+ feet — significantly colder than Zion. Late March temperatures drop to the 20s°F overnight, low 50s during the day. Layers are non-negotiable: wool base, puffy, wind shell. Don't assume Zion weather equals Bryce weather.

Day 6 Stay in Bryce

Tuesday, March 31

The hoodoo masterpiece.

Morning

Navajo Loop / Queens Garden

3.0 mi Moderate

The definitive Bryce hike — descend through Wall Street (narrow slot between hoodoos towering overhead), wind through the amphitheater floor among the formations, and exit past the Queen Victoria hoodoo. The pro move: go down Navajo, up Queens Garden. It's the right direction and turns a hard climb into an easy one.

View on AllTrails →
Afternoon

Mossy Cave Trail

0.8 mi Easy

Bryce's most underrated gem — an easy out-and-back to a waterfall and a grotto dripping with moss and occasional early-spring ice formations. Located just outside the main park on Hwy 12. Free, no shuttle needed, and completely unique from everything else in the park. Kids love the waterfall.

View on AllTrails →
Drive the Park Road Between Hikes

Natural Bridge Overlook + Rainbow Point

Drive the 17-mile park road south to Natural Bridge overlook — a massive stone arch framing the canyon below it — then continue to Rainbow Point at 9,115 feet, the highest viewpoint in Bryce. On a clear day you can see into four states. Takes 45 minutes round trip with zero hiking required.

🌅

Set your alarm for Sunrise Point. Bryce is one of the rare places where sunrise beats sunset — the low morning angle lights the hoodoos from within in a way that looks almost unreal. Around 6:30 AM in late March. Bring your warmest layer.

Day 7 Bryce → Torrey · Highway 12

Wednesday, April 1

Highway 12 — the most beautiful drive in America.

🏨 Red Sands Hotel · Torrey · 1 night
Highway 12 Scenic Byway · ~2.5 Hours

The Drive That Makes People Pull Over and Cry

Highway 12 from Bryce to Torrey is not marketing — it is genuinely one of the most breathtaking stretches of road in the United States. It climbs over Boulder Mountain, descends through slot canyon country, and crosses The Hogback, a razor-thin ridge where the road is barely two lanes wide with 800-foot drops on both sides. Slow down. Build in 90 extra minutes for stops. You will not regret it.

☕ Kiva Koffeehouse — canyon rim coffee, must stop 🌉 The Hogback — pull over and stand on the ridge 🏔️ Boynton Overlook — sweeping canyon panorama 🏺 Anasazi State Park — Boulder (15 min, worth it) 💧 Calf Creek Falls viewpoint — roadside pull-off
Can't-Miss Stop · On Your Exact Route

☕ Kiva Koffeehouse

A coffee shop cantilevered over a canyon rim between Escalante and Boulder — cappuccino with a 600-foot drop below the windows. Cinnamon rolls, fresh pastries, and arguably the best view from any coffee shop anywhere. Seasonal — opens mid-March most years, confirm hours before you go. This is not optional.

Afternoon · Capitol Reef National Park

Cassidy Arch Trail

3.4 mi Moderate

Named for Butch Cassidy, who used Capitol Reef's canyon network as a hideout. The trail climbs above Grand Wash to a massive sandstone arch — and you can walk across it. Views from the top span the entire Waterpocket Fold. One of the most undervisited great hikes in Utah's national parks system, which means almost no crowds.

View on AllTrails →
🥧

Gifford House is inside Capitol Reef and opens early spring — call ahead to confirm April hours. Their homemade pies (apple, cherry, peach) and preserves sell out by mid-morning. Plan to swing by first thing on 4/2 before leaving for Moab.

Day 8 Torrey → Moab · Hwy 24

Thursday, April 2

Alien landscapes. Best drive nobody talks about.

🏡 Moab VRBO · Check-in · 3 nights
The Hanksville Trio · Highway 24 East

Factory Butte, Bentonite Hills & Moonscape Overlook

Highway 24 east of Capitol Reef enters a landscape that looks genuinely extraterrestrial — pale grey and purple clay badlands, flat-topped mesas rising from white plains, zero vegetation for miles. Pull off for Factory Butte (10 minutes on a well-maintained dirt road), the colored Bentonite Hills, and the sweeping Moonscape Overlook. Mars, Utah-edition. Don't rush through this stretch.

🏔️ Factory Butte — 10 min dirt road off Hwy 24 🎨 Bentonite Hills — purple and grey alien badlands 🌐 Moonscape Overlook — sweeping extraterrestrial views ⛽ Hollow Mountain — store literally carved into sandstone 👾 Goblin Valley — 30 min detour, absolutely worth it
30-Minute Detour Off Hwy 24 — Highly Recommended

👾 Goblin Valley State Park

Hundreds of mushroom-shaped sandstone goblins clustered in a flat valley with no designated trails — you just wander among them. Used as a film location, beloved by every family who's ever been there. $20/vehicle entry. One of Utah's most unique landscapes and wildly undervisited compared to the national parks. Kids go absolutely feral here in the best way.

Morning · Before Leaving Torrey

Gifford House

Must Stop

Start the day here, inside Capitol Reef. Homemade pies, fresh jams, and preserves in a historic pioneer homestead. Get there early — they bake daily but supply is limited and it sells out fast. Grab two pies: one for the road today, one for tonight in Moab.

Order: Apple pie, cherry pie, grab the preserves too
Afternoon · Arches National Park

Delicate Arch

3.2 mi Moderate

Utah's most iconic image — a 65-foot freestanding arch on a slickrock bowl with the La Sal Mountains behind it. The hike feels ordinary until you crest the final ridge and it's suddenly just there, enormous and impossible. Go for sunset. The arch turns deep amber and the crowd goes quiet. It earns every bit of its reputation.

View on AllTrails →
🔦

Every person needs a headlamp for Delicate Arch. Go at sunset — the arch turns amber and the experience is incomparable. But the slickrock descent back to the trailhead is in full dark, and phone flashlights don't cut it. Real headlamps, everyone, including the kids.

Day 9 Stay in Moab

Friday, April 3

Glowing arch. Canyon infinity. The view that ends all arguments.

Sunrise — Set Your Alarm

Mesa Arch

0.6 mi Easy

The most photogenic 20 minutes of the entire trip. A flat half-mile walk to a sandstone arch that frames the canyon below — at sunrise, the underside of the arch reflects the canyon's glow and lights up fiery orange. Be there 20 minutes before sunrise. You'll understand immediately why this photograph exists everywhere.

View on AllTrails →
Afternoon

Grand View Point Trail

2.0 mi Easy

A flat, exposed rim walk to the southernmost overlook in Canyonlands' Island in the Sky district. The entire canyon system spreads below you for 100 miles in every direction. The Colorado and Green Rivers are visible 2,000 feet down as silver ribbons through the rock. There are no guardrails. The scale is genuinely disorienting.

View on AllTrails →
30-Minute Detour — Absolutely Do Not Skip

Dead Horse Point State Park

A narrow mesa jutting 2,000 feet over a perfect Colorado River horseshoe bend. Many seasoned travelers say this beats the Grand Canyon's South Rim for sheer visual impact per square foot of viewing area. It's just off the road to Canyonlands Island in the Sky — you're essentially driving past it anyway. $20 state park fee. Go.

🗺️

Mesa Arch at sunrise → Dead Horse Point mid-morning → Grand View Point after lunch. These three stops are within 30 minutes of each other and flow perfectly in that order. The whole day feels effortless and the payoffs are enormous.

Bigger than Delicate Arch — tall enough for a Boeing 747 to fly through (people have actually done it). On the Drive Back from Corona Arch

Fisher Towers + Highway 128 River Road

Take Highway 128 along the Colorado River out of Moab instead of the highway — red canyon walls, cottonwood groves, and Castle Valley spires the whole way. Pull off at Fisher Towers (massive 900-foot red sandstone spires used in Westerns and car commercials for decades). Five-minute stop, completely unforgettable silhouette against the sky.

Little Egypt Geological Site — Your Farewell Gift

On Highway 24 heading back toward I-70 and SLC, pull off for Little Egypt Geological Site — a cluster of small sandstone hoodoos rising from the desert floor, completely free, completely roadside. Springdale

Gateway to Zion · Surprisingly solid food and coffee scene for a small town

☕ Best Coffee

Deep Creek Coffee Co.

Reddit's unanimous Springdale coffee pick, year after year. Solid espresso drinks, outdoor river seating, and the rosemary focaccia is genuinely excellent. Gets busy by 8 AM — go early or plan for a short wait. Worth every minute.

Order: Cappuccino + rosemary focaccia
🌮 Best Breakfast / Lunch

Oscar's Cafe

Springdale institution since 1993. Trail runners and rangers swear by the massive pork green chili burritos — portions are enormous and the price is right. Breakfast is solid. Lines move fast. Go here before or after your first shuttle ride.

Order: Pork green chili burrito, huevos rancheros
🍹 Best Dinner

Bit & Spur Restaurant

Springdale's best sit-down dinner. Upscale Mexican-inspired with great margaritas, a lively patio, and food that punches well above its desert-town location. Beloved by the guide and ranger community. Reservations recommended on weekends.

Order: Tamales, enchilada plates, a mezcal margarita
🌵 Local Go-To

Whiptail Grill

Casual Mexican with a big outdoor patio and mountain views. Great for groups, relaxed atmosphere, solid tacos. The hiking crowd's pick for eating outside after a trail day. Seasonal — call ahead to confirm they're open in late March.

Veteran Advice

Know Before You Go

🤿

Rent Dry Suits for the Narrows

Non-negotiable in March. Water temps hit 40–50°F — without dry suit and neoprene socks you risk hypothermia within an hour. Rent from Zion Outfitter or Zion Adventure Company (~$45/person). Reserve online before arriving in Springdale.

🔦

Headlamp for Delicate Arch

Go at sunset — the arch turns amber and nothing compares. But the slickrock descent back is in full dark. Every person needs a real headlamp (not a phone flashlight). Include the kids.

🎒

Pack Your Own Lunches

Zion shuttle food lines can hit 45–60 minutes at peak times. Packed lunches mean you eat when and where you want — on a canyon rim, at the river, at the trailhead. Stock up your first night in SLC. Life-changing for a family trip.

🥧

Gifford House Pies Sell Out by 10 AM

They bake daily but supply is limited. If you arrive at noon there's nothing left. Go first thing in the morning — this applies especially in early April as they ramp up for the season.

🌡️

Bryce is Much Colder Than Zion

Bryce is at 8,000 feet. Zion is at 4,000 feet. Nights drop to the 20s°F. Pack real layers — wool base, puffy jacket, wind shell. Don't assume Zion weather equals Bryce weather. It doesn't.

🌊

Check Narrows Conditions Every Morning

The Narrows closes instantly when upstream weather threatens floods. Check zionpark.net the morning of your hike. Backup plan: Emerald Pools loop (Shuttle Stop 5) is excellent and keeps everyone happy.

🌅

Bryce Sunrise Beats Sunset

Set an alarm for Sunrise Point — low morning light makes the hoodoos glow from within in a way sunset cannot replicate. Around 6:30 AM in late March. Bring your warmest layer and don't skip it.

📸

Mesa Arch: Arrive 20 Minutes Early

The arch glows for about 10 minutes at sunrise. Arrive 20 minutes before sunrise — photographers will already be there, but it's still extraordinary. This is the single best alarm-worthy moment of the whole trip.

💧

Hydrate Before You're Thirsty

Utah's desert air removes moisture faster than you feel it. Plan 1 liter per person per 2 hours of hiking. Altitude at Bryce and Canyonlands makes this worse. Start the morning hydrated, not catching up.
